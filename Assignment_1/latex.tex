\section{Problem}
Given that
\begin{align}
p_X(x) =\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \exp\brak{- \frac{x^2}{2}}, -\infty <x< \infty
\end{align}
Find the mean and variane theoritically.
\section{Solution}
Clearly, this is the pdf of Gaussian distribution with mean 0 and variance 1.\\
\begin{align}
    E(X) &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x e^{-\frac{x^2}{2}}dx\\
    &=0 \quad \brak{\because odd function}
\end{align}
\begin{align}
    E(X^2)&= \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x^2
e^ {-\frac{x^2}{2}} dx \quad \brak{even function}\\
    &= \frac{2}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \int_{0}^{\infty} x^2 e^{-\frac{x^2}{2}} dx\\
    &= \frac{2}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{0}^{\infty}\sqrt{2u}.e^{-u} du \quad\brak{Let \frac{x^2}{2}= u}\\
    &= \frac{2}{\sqrt{\pi}} \int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-u}. u^{\frac{3}{2}-1} du\\
    &= \frac{2}{\sqrt{\pi}}. \Gamma\brak{{\frac{3}{2}}}\\
    &= \frac{2}{\sqrt{\pi}}.\frac{1}{2}.\Gamma\brak{\frac{1}{2}} \quad\because \Gamma(n)= (n-1)\Gamma(n-1); \Gamma\brak{\frac{1}{2}}=\sqrt{\pi}\\
    &= 1
\end{align}
Thus Variance is:
\begin{align}
    V\brak X &= E\brak X^2 - E^2\brak X\\
    &= 1- 0\\
    & = 1
\end{align}
Thus, theoritically the mean and variance of the given distribution is 0 and 1 respectively, as seen in the C program computationally
